Jackson Fan : 07. NZXWT Staff fee vs. when staff not staff charged Normal fee Data Reconciliation

Created by Jackson Fan, last modified on 20-08-24

Problem Statement:

Ant and Diane have raised additional requests regarding data reconciliation for the staff fee report. Ant compiled a complete list of portfolios with the same fee packages and compared it to the staff fee report, identifying a 5% variance due to missing portfolios.

It is important to note that Ant's list includes all portfolios with the same fee packages, encompassing closed accounts and those that were active in 2020.

This raises questions about the business purpose of the report and the necessity of including all historical portfolios.

Given that we are only querying data for a specific time period, it is reasonable to expect some missing portfolios, as those outside this timeframe should not appear in the report.

Ant mentioned he would double-check with Diane for clarification.

While I plan to investigate the root causes of the missing portfolios, I am skeptical that we will achieve a 100% match due to the nature of the data and the time period being analyzed.

Next Steps for Business Analyst (BA):

  1. Clarify Business Purpose: Engage with Ant and Diane to understand the specific objectives of the staff fee report and the rationale for including all historical portfolios.

  2. Analyze Data Queries: Review the data queries used to generate the staff fee report to ensure they align with the defined time period and business requirements.

  3. Investigate Missing Portfolios: Conduct a detailed analysis to identify the root causes of the missing portfolios, including:

    • Data entry errors

    • Differences in portfolio status (active vs. closed)

    • Time period discrepancies

  4. Document Findings: Compile the findings and insights from the analysis to present to Ant and Diane, highlighting any limitations and the potential for variance in the data.

  5. Recommendations: Based on the analysis, provide recommendations for improving data reconciliation processes and ensuring that future reports meet business needs effectively.
