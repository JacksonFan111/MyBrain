    Understanding the Issue

    • Expected Behavior: The Controlling Person's TIN2 (sah.TIN2) should be sourced from the Controlling Person's individual foreign tax records in CRM.

    • Actual Behavior: The Entity's TIN (ft.cip_Identification) is showing up as the Controlling Person's TIN2, even when the Controlling Person does not have a TIN2 loaded in their CRM record.

    Tracing the Data Flow

    You've correctly traced the issue through the various steps of your query:

    • The join condition in Step 5 does not sufficiently distinguish between Entity TINs and Controlling Person TINs.

    • As a result, the Entity TIN (ft.cip_Identification) is incorrectly associated with the Controlling Person's TIN2 (sah.TIN2).

    Understanding Layered Entities in CRM

    In (CRM), a Layered Entity allows for the representation of complex relationships between individuals and their roles across different organizational levels. Here’s a breakdown of how this works, using the example of an individual named Sarah:

    1. Multiple Roles for One Individual:

      • An individual can hold multiple roles within different entities. For instance, Sarah can be both an "Authorised Person" at the Parent Entity level and a "Director" at the Child Entity level.

    2. Role Visibility:

      • The roles assigned to an individual may not be visible at all levels. For example:

        • At the Parent Entity level, Sarah's role as the "Authorised Person" is visible.

        • However, her role as the "Director" of the Child Entity is only visible when you drill down to the Child Entity level.

    3. Concatenating Information for Clarity:

      • To uniquely identify the lowest grain of data regarding an individual’s role, you can concatenate the following elements:

        • Account ID: The identifier for the organization or entity.

        • Individual ID: The identifier for the individual (in this case, Sarah).

        • Role ID: The identifier for the specific role held by the individual.

      This concatenation provides a clear and unique representation of the individual’s role within the context of the organization. Here are some examples:

      • For Sarah as the "Authorised Person" of the Parent Entity:

        • Concatenated Result: abcFUND + Sarah + Authorised Person of abcFUND

      • For Sarah as the "Director" of the Child Entity:

        • Concatenated Result: abcFundchild Entity + Sarah + Director of abcFundchild Entity

      • For Sarah as the "Accountant" of another entity:

        • Concatenated Result: CoolAccounting + Sarah + Accountant of CoolAccounting

    Background on Changes to View [FATCA].[vw_ForeignTax_Entities]

    Problem 1: Incorrect Filtering Logic

    The current implementation uses the field sm2.AttributeName = 'dsl_passiveoractive' for joins and filters, which appears to be derived from the FATCA/W-8 section. In contrast, the CRS (Common Reporting Standard) relies on the FATCA section Flag for data filtering. This discrepancy leads to the incorrect identification of entities.

    Problem 2: Layered Entity Scenario Not Addressed

    The view does not account for layered entity scenarios. For example, consider the following structure:

    • Parent Entity: abcFUND

    • Child Entity: abcFundchild (Corporate Trustee of abcFUND)

    • Individual: Sarah (Director of abcFundchild)

    In this case, abcFUND is classified as a Passive Non-Financial Entity (NFE), and Sarah is the controlling person from a CRS perspective. The concatenated results for different roles are as follows:

    • As Authorised Person of Parent Entity: abcFUND + Sarah + Authorised Person of abcFUND

    • As Director of Child Entity: abcFundchild + Sarah + Director of abcFundchild

    • As Accountant of Another Entity: CoolAccounting + Sarah + Accountant of CoolAccounting

    Due to the layered structure, the query returns a NULL value for i.FullName at the account level, as it pertains to a child entity. To address this, a flag and a unique ID have been created to indicate which role to use in the CRS layered entity scenario.

    Proposed Solution

    A flag has been implemented to describe the role and ownership, using the following logic:

    CASE
