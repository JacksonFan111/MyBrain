Key Takeaways

  1. Hist Suffix
    All these collections/tables appear to be historical snapshots of the primary “live” data, capturing changes over time or storing audit/point-in-time records.

  2. __dbt_tmp Suffix
    Indicates temporary staging tables created by dbt. They often mirror the structure of the final table but are used mid-process to handle transformations, data merges, or incremental loads.

  3. Domain Grouping

    • Fee: Historical invoices, schedules, and fee details.

    • Performance: Historical benchmarks, indices, and associated components.

    • Payment: Historical payment orders and placements.

    • Reference: Historical snapshots of account-level reference data.

    • Transaction / Valuation (Thv): Historical transaction/income definitions and detail lines.

By grouping them this way, you get a clearer picture of which part of the system each historical table belongs to, and how they might link back to the “live” (non-historical) collections.
