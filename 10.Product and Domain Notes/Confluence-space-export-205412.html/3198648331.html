Jackson Fan : 1.Funds Flow — One-Page Table Brief

Created by Jackson Fan, last modified on 08-06-25

1 Why this table exists

Tracks the money moving in and out of each client account over a reporting period (Usually one Quarter) so advisers, branch managers, and Finance can answer:

  • “How much net cash did we raise (or lose) this month?” – (Net Flow)

  • “Which branches or advisers are driving flows?” – (Who is contributing?)

  • “What portion of performance came from market gains vs. client activity?”

The table underpins FUM-flow dashboards (Power BI) and feeds KPI scorecards tied to adviser remuneration.

3 Row grain (very important)

One row per (AccountKey, DateKey), Each Clients acccount and each date

  • DateKey = period identifier returned by the view (usually calendar month end).

  • Financial columns (Opening MV, Net Contributions, Gains, Fees …) are already period totals; they should not be re-aggregated across DateKey without a time filter.

6 Design considerations & risks

Observation

Why it matters

Suggested practice

Money stored as decimal but sourced from double

Avoids floating-point drift once inside model.

Keep decimal in ETL too.

DateKey is string

Requires explicit relationship to Dates table.

Create calculated column Date = DATEVALUE(DateKey).

Many hidden base columns

Reduces report clutter.

Expose only curated measures to self-service users.

Full-table refresh

View scans entire fact; heavy at FY close.

Optionally switch to incremental partitions on Run Date.

7 TL;DR

Funds Flow is the account-period fact that reconciles opening market value to closing value by showing every dollar of client cash movement, income, fees and market performance—making it the backbone for FUM growth, retention, and adviser performance reporting.
