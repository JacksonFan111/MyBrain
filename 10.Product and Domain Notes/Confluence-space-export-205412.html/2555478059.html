Jackson Fan : Project Cirrus update (Craigs Microsoft Azure cloud migration)

Created by Jackson Fan on 12-06-24

What is it Project Cirrus?

  • Cirrus is the project migrating Craigs Digital infrastructure (over 110 servers) to Microsoft’s Cloud services (Azure).

  • This is the largest Digital infrastructure Project undertaken in many years, with 3 Craigs Infrastructure Team members 100% allocated (as well as many other Digital Business staff) working alongside 3 fulltime LAB3 resources (our Cloud Partner) to plan and execute the Project.

  • Once completed, most of our Craigs applications will reside on servers located in Microsoft’s Australia East Datacenter.

 

Why a move to Cloud for Craigs?

 

  • Getting off aging server hardware/software and shutting down expensive Datacentre equipment and location(s) to provide operational savings.

  • Enabling a highly secure, highly scalable, and flexible IT infrastructure, using code to automate the rapid deployment of Cloud infrastructure.

  • Access to new (Cloud only) Technology – e.g. Co-Pilot / AI etc.

  • An enhanced DR/BCP proposition for Craigs.

  • A solution that provides a sustainability approach for Craigs Digital infrastructure moving forward, leveraging off Microsoft’s market leading practices and reporting capability in this area.

 

Where are we at?

 

  • There are 5 “waves” of migrations taking place in evenings or weekends.

    • 4 have already been successfully completed.

  • On the weekend of 21st, 22nd and 23rd of June we will be undertaking our final Azure migration Wave. This will see the final 29 servers migrated to Azure and includes the main Craigs business applications such as CRM, SharePoint Documents, Client Portal, Bob2 just to name a few.

 

What do you need to know?

 

  • On the above dates, all Craigs Applications will be unavailable until the migration is complete.

    • We appreciate this is an extended outage, but we need to ensure all applications migrate with no issues.

    • Please exit all applications prior to 5pm on Friday evening and do not attempt to use these during this period.

    • Communication will be sent to the business once these are available again.

  • The Craigs Portal will also be unavailable during the migration and until data integrity has been confirmed – a notice of this will be posted soon and a holding message will be posted (instead of the CIP login page).

  • We want to ensure that there are no surprises come Monday; so, like in our BCP tests, we will be asking some of you to test applications – where it is not possible for Digital Business to do this on your behalf (such as Fees, Reporting and Dealing). Your assistance is appreciated, and we will be in touch in the next 24 hours regarding your input and when it may be required.

  • On Monday, you should notice no negative impacts. If you do notice any issues on Monday, please log a Service Portal request to advise. If the request is urgent, please ensure you call the Service Desk to escalate.

 

Any questions, please reach out directly. A reminder of the outages will be sent closer to the time.
