Why adding people clarifies—but also complicates—the picture

  1. Multiple owners per hop
    Each system box (A‑E) has different humans controlling data logic vs job timing vs infrastructure, so responsibility diffuses.

  2. Conflicting incentives
    - Finance wants speed & transparency (Ben, Andrew).
    - Vendor protects IP (Kelly, Reena).
    - Data‑team caught in‑between (Imran, Cat).

  3. Sentiment mismatch leaks into tech dialogue
    Defensive vendor tone slows answers; CFO urgency accelerates questions—creates a conversational impedance mismatch.

  4. The real ‘troublemaker’ is just a job, not a person
    Personifying the Closed‑Expired‑Orders job helps the narrative, but the job itself is scheduled by Reena inside Chelmer; nobody else can see its timing until after the fact.

  5. Human memory vs asynchronous tickets
    When half the actors join late, they re‑ask context; the thread balloons and the logical flow (A → F) gets lost.
