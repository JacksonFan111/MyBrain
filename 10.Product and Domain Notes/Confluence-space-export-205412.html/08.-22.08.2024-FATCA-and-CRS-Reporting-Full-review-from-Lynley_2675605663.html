  • How do we determine the controlling persons on a passive NFE in CRM?- Reporting values need to go against each if they are FTR?

    • ANSWER: the business / onboarding team are responsible for this and track it on a field in CRM.

  • What is the Controlling person ownership % for passive NFEs (script = 10%; CRS = 25%).

    • ANSWER: % ownership is not a main component of determining controlling persons for passive NFEs - however, CRS does provide guidance of what that % could look like. Not sure where the current SQL script for the FATCA/CRS report gets 10% from (CRS = 25%).

  • Difference in CRS / FATCA reports when an account is closed (CRS - no balance information included, FATCA - last balance / value before account was closed is reported)

  • Where an account is jointly held, each holder of the account is attributed the entire balance or value of the joint account as well as the entire amounts paid or credited to the joint account. Same principle applies to each of the controlling persons on a passive NFE.

  • Accounts with negative balance must be reported as having a zero balance.

  • Where an account is held by an account holder that is a Reportable Person who is identified as having more than one jurisdiction of residence, the entire balance or value of the account, as well as the entire amount paid or credited to the account must be reported with respect to each jurisdiction of residence of the account holder. This principle also applies to controlling persons of passive NFEs.

  • Both passive NFE (if FTR) as well as each of the Controlling Persons that are FTR will have the entire balance or value of the account held by the Passive NFE as well as the entire amount paid or credited to the account reported.

  • For FATCA - if passive NFE is US person - then no need to look into whether the Controlling Persons are US citizens (i.e. only look into the Controlling Persons when the passive NFE is not a US person). However, if there are US FTR that are Controlling persons with regards to a non-US passive NFE, then both the NFE and the Controlling persons must be reported to FATCA.

FATCA

  • Para 3-4 page 22 - NPFI (aggregation of reporting)

  • Pg 23 - where is NPFI payments being reported today? How do we deal with this going forward?

  • Has account aggregation rules: determined on aggregations of values of accounts linked to a client across subsidiaries (see page 26-28). Exemption rules are based on account type and value as well as total aggregate value.

    • E.G. if a client has multiple custodial accounts (including joint accounts) - but not exempt accounts (like Kiwisaver), and the sum of those accounts is less than US$50k then none are reportable, however if the aggregate value exceeds US$50K, even where none of the custodial accounts exceed the minimum threshold; all of them will be reportable if the client is a US citizen.

    • Threshold values need to be converted from US$ to NZ$ threshold values by using spot rate determined as of the last day of the calendar year preceding the year in which the account balance or value was determined.

Key Issues Identified

  1. Country Codes:

    • Incorrect formatting of country codes (e.g., Bonaire, South Korea, Taiwan).

    • Missing country codes for addresses.

  2. Entity Information:

    • Entity names not consistently pulling through onto reports.

    • Active entities and Financial Institutions (FIs) not being distinguished, leading to manual removals.

    • Controlling persons' details missing for NZ entities.

  3. Tax Identification Numbers (TINs):

    • Incorrect TINs appearing as second TINs.

    • TINs not being pulled from CRM correctly.

    • US SSNs appearing in CRS reports, which should be excluded.

  4. Report Updates:

    • Reports not reflecting new accounts onboarded during the financial year, even after being primed.

    • Manual adjustments required for controlling person country codes and addresses.

  5. FATCA Report Issues:

    • Missing Filer/Sponsored Entity Short Name.

    • Operation Type now represented as a letter instead of a number.

    • Manual adjustments needed for Account Holder Tax Jurisdiction Code and TIN for NZ trusts and companies.

    • Incorrect Reportable Account Type numbers requiring manual changes.

    • Errors due to accented characters or incorrect symbols causing issues in the error log.

  6. Submission Process:

    • The Sending Company IRD number has changed, requiring manual updates for each report.

  7. New FATCA Template Changes:

    • Aggregate Account Balance column not accepting data.

    • New requirement for the number of accounts.

    • Column headings have changed, leading to potential data gaps.
